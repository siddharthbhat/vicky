Sensor Information




Temperature Sensor

The temperature sensor measures the ambient temperature across the campus. This data helps in regulating heating and cooling systems to ensure comfort for students and staff, as well as optimizing energy usage.

Humidity Sensor

The humidity sensor tracks the moisture level in the air. It is crucial for maintaining optimal indoor air quality, preventing mold growth, and ensuring the comfort and health of occupants.

Noise Level Sensor

The noise level sensor monitors the ambient noise levels in different areas of the campus. This data helps identify noisy environments, allowing for better design and management of spaces for study, work, and relaxation.

Occupancy Sensor

The occupancy sensor detects the presence of people in various rooms and spaces. It helps in optimizing space utilization, improving building security, and enhancing the overall campus experience for students, staff, and visitors.

Parking Sensor

Installed in parking areas, these sensors can provide real-time data on parking availability, helping to reduce congestion and improve the parking experience for students, staff, and visitors.

Energy Sensor

Information from sensors and smart meters monitoring the energy usage of buildings, lighting, and other campus facilities. This data can help in identifying patterns, peak usage times, and opportunities for energy saving and sustainability initiatives.
